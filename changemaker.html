Why?

Hyperlocal volunteering is centered on a well-defined community with its primary focus directed towards the concerns of the population in that community.
We often get to think of many solutions to resolve some of the pressing issues in our own immediate environments.However, certain factors prevent us from initiating and moving ahead.
VolunteerGram is a comprehensive support platform that enable volunteers to take charge of their own ideas, innovate in their own ways, and build a group of doers to achieve their bigger social goal through volunteering.

Who can participate?

Anybody passionate about creating a change in the society can join us through this venture. Those with a project idea can apply through our website.
Others who are interested may join in any of the initiatives they would like to volunteer in. Team VolunteerGram will also support in onboarding to any of the groups.

How?

VolunteerGram is being launched as a campaign through which passionate volunteers can bring up their hyperlocal impact ideas through our website.
You may apply with your group members or individually to later form a team. Selected ones will get a chance to execute their ideas into impactful projects under the mentorship, guidance & support of experts.
If you do not have an idea, Don’t worry! We can help you build one. If you do not want to build one, you can still volunteer with any one of the existing groups.

About the Project

Kites is introducing ‘VolunteerGram’with an objective of building a movement of youth-led hyperlocal initiatives to nurture future leaders and changemakers by providing requisite resources and support to put their ideas into action. Through the initiative we aspire to create a positive impact in the lives of people at the grassroots level since volunteering is giving, sharing, standing by others, supporting causes you care about and creating a better future for everyone. Here is a unique opportunity to make an impact, learn, network and improve your social skills.

Join VolunteerGram and be part of a change-making story!

1. Releasing the registration form, poster and brochure, along with promotional content through social media platforms.

2. One to one approach to volunteers and colleges to create awareness about the campaign.

3. Collecting inputs from the selected applicants through a PowerPoint template.

4. One to one interaction with the further shortlisted registrants.

5. The final shortlisted ones can form their teams. If not, Kites will be allocating interested volunteers depending on the projects and required members. Each team can have a minimum of 10 members to a maximum of 15. Each team will have two leads.

6. Offline workshop with proper guidance to each team, along with a separate mentor. The mentor will be responsible for guiding the team throughout the project.

7. Each team should visit the Kites office every Sunday and share their updates on Friday through an online meeting.

8. Each team will have 15-20 days to execute the project. The team should parallelly prepare their project report and other creative ways to present the project and its impact. In the end, there will be a meeting with an eminent jury panel to analyse each project, and the best-selected teams will be rewarded.
