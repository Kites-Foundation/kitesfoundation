-Rakhi Raveendran

It was really amazing to be a part of summer pot campaign,and the real fun was to make a toddler,the naughty Vihaan, a part of it too...
My mom used to show him the birds and small animals like cat, dog,squrrels as it was the only trick to open up his mouth to fed meals.He was too curious about how they move especially their flight,their voice and even started to imitate some of them.
So I decided to join in this campaign just to familiarize my baby with nature.First i placed one plastic basket above the compound wall and one tied in the leant branches of mango tree so that we could access it from the open terrace.I filled it with water along with some wheet bells by carrying him in my hand.. Little Vihaan curiouslyly observed these activities and his responds brought me immense pleasure.his eyes shined bright as them approaching the pot , he started to smile and murmer in his own language..after that I actively participated him by arranging some potteries to his right and allowed to fill the water himself.Vihaan realy enjoyed this activities because of somany reasons, it was his golden opportunity to play with water too.
My parents supported him to make this a routine ,amma helped him to provide wheet bells and cooked rice so that they quench thirst and hunger.My baby eagerly waited for their arrival and started to clap happily when they approched his pot. Me too was very happy and proud as my son was interested in the nature's offsprings. br> It might not be there in his memmory for long but am sure this small task may influence him in any manner and will helps to connect him in future with the mother nature...
